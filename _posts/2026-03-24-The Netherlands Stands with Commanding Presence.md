## I. The Cultural Context: The Lion of Orange and the Oranje Legacy of Resilience

The Dutch lion—known as the "Leeuw van Oranje"—traces its roots deep into heraldry and national identity. Emerging from the House of Orange-Nassau and the historic Dutch Republic Lion, it has symbolized courage, sovereignty, and unbowed pride since the 16th century. Adopted onto football crests as early as 1907, the lion became synonymous with the Oranje, the nickname derived from the royal family's princely title and the iconic orange worn by the team.

In the lead-up to World Cup 2026, the Netherlands' qualification—clinching top spot in Group G with dominant performances—underscores this enduring spirit.
