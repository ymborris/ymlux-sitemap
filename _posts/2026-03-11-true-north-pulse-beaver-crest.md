## I. The Cultural Anchor: Beaver as Symbol of Canadian Resilience

The beaver's place in Canadian identity runs deep. Once nearly driven to extinction by fur trade demands, it rebounded through conservation—a testament to quiet determination and adaptability. Chosen as national animal in 1975, it embodies industrious spirit, engineering prowess, and the ability to thrive in harsh environments. The maple leaf, iconic on the flag since 1965, adds layers of unity and natural beauty.

Football in Canada has mirrored this journey. The men's national team qualified for its first World Cup in 1986, a historic breakthrough, though progress was slow. The 2022 Qatar appearance—Canada's second ever—brought renewed momentum, with Alphonso Davies' electric presence and a generation of talent emerging from MLS academies and European leagues. Now, as co-host in 2026, Canada stands on the cusp of something transformative. Playing on home soil for the first time, facing Qatar in Vancouver and Switzerland again at BC Place, Les Rouges carry the weight of a nation's expectations blended with the joy of firsts.
