### The Financial Payoff: Inter Miami’s Investment in Messi Delivers Massive Returns

When Inter Miami signed Lionel Messi in July 2023, many viewed it as a high-risk gamble for a young expansion club. Pre-Messi, the club generated approximately $56 million in revenue in 2022. By the 2025 season, revenue had surged to roughly $200 million — a nearly 300% increase in just three years.

Club valuation tells an even more dramatic story. Before Messi’s arrival, Inter Miami was valued around $585–600 million. By 2026, Forbes estimates place the franchise at $1.35 billion, making it the most valuable club in MLS and surpassing previous leaders like LAFC. This surge reflects explosive growth in ticket sales, sponsorships, global tours, Apple TV subscriptions, and especially merchandise.

The “Messi Effect” extended well beyond on-field performances. Matchday attendance surged league-wide when Inter Miami visited, with several away games drawing record crowds exceeding 70,000. Home games consistently sold out, with premium seating and hospitality packages commanding higher prices. International preseason tours and commercial partnerships (including major deals with Adidas and Apple) added significant revenue streams.

Crucially, the club’s heavy spending on Messi and supporting stars proved self-funding. Messi’s contract structure cleverly included equity options and revenue-sharing elements, allowing Inter Miami to offset a substantial portion of his compensation through increased commercial income. The gamble paid off handsomely: revenue nearly quadrupled while the club achieved healthy positive operating income, funding further investments without straining ownership resources.

### How Messi’s Arrival Attracted More Stars and Boosted Jersey Sales

Messi’s signing created a virtuous cycle. His presence didn’t just sell tickets and boost brand visibility — it made Inter Miami an attractive destination for other elite players, many of them former Barcelona teammates seeking reunion and competitive success.

Sergio Busquets and Jordi Alba joined shortly after Messi in 2023. Luis Suárez arrived in 2024, adding lethal finishing. These arrivals were not coincidental; players openly cited the opportunity to play alongside Messi and the winning culture he helped instill as key factors.

The impact on merchandise was immediate and sustained. Messi’s pink No. 10 Inter Miami jersey topped MLS jersey sales in 2023, 2024, and 2025. In 2025, Inter Miami players dominated the top 25 best-selling jerseys: Messi at No. 1, Suárez at No. 2, Busquets in the top 16, and academy product Benjamin Cremaschi also charting high. Overall MLS jersey sales rose significantly, with a large portion of that growth directly attributable to the Miami surge.

The pink-and-black aesthetic became a cultural phenomenon. Fans worldwide sought not only Messi’s kit but also supporting stars’ jerseys, creating a multiplier effect that translated into millions in additional revenue while strengthening fan engagement across the U.S., Canada, and international markets.
